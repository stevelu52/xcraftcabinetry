Cabinetry Product Policy

Wood and Material Composition

Wood is a natural product that expands and contracts in response to temperature and humidity fluctuations. This seasonal movement is a normal and unavoidable characteristic of wood materials.

As a result, small gaps (typically 1mm–3mm) may appear or close along joints, seams, or mitered corners of cabinet doors. These shifts are natural characteristics of wood and are not considered defects. Such variations are not covered under warranty.

Natural variations in wood color, grain patterns, mineral streaks, and knots are inherent to wood and do not qualify as defects.

Maple wood features a subtle grain pattern with a uniform appearance. Its color ranges from creamy white to pale red/brown and may include bird's eye dots and mineral streaks. These natural characteristics are not defects.

Engineered materials (MDF, particle board, veneers, etc.) may behave differently than solid wood. Minor surface or finish variations may occur and are likewise not considered defects.

Natural Stone Policy

Marble, Granite, Quartzite

Order Terms

Natural stone countertops are special order products: no returns, no refunds.

Square footage and pricing are subject to change after templating.

Full payment is required to place the order.

Characteristics of Natural Stone

Stone is a natural product; variations in color, veining, movement, and tonality are normal and expected.

These variations occur from slab to slab and lot to lot and cannot be controlled.

Fissures, pitting, chips, and polish irregularities are inherent to natural stone and must be accepted.

Thickness is nominal and may vary ±3mm.

Seams are required and may vary ±2mm. No seam is invisible.

These natural features are considered part of the beauty of stone, not defects.

Customer Responsibilities Before Template

  • All selections (stone, color, edge detail, sink, faucet holes, appliances, cutouts) must be finalized prior to templating.
  • The customer must view and approve the actual slab before fabrication.
  • Cabinets must be fully leveled and permanently secured before templating.
  • Farm/apron sinks must be installed, fully supported, and in final position before templating.
  • The customer must disconnect and reconnect plumbing, gas, and electrical.
  • All sinks, faucets, stoves, and cooktops must be on site at the time of templating.
  • The customer must remove existing countertops and backsplash before templating.
  • No changes may be made after the template is completed.

Sealant and Maintenance Requirements

  • Honed and leather finishes: Must be sealed twice per year by a professional.
  • Polished light-colored stones (lower density): Must be sealed 3–4 times per year.
  • Polished dark stones (higher density): Must be sealed 1–2 times per year.
  • The customer is solely responsible for ongoing sealing and maintenance of stone surfaces.

Quartz Policy

Order Terms

Quartz countertops are special order products: no returns, no refunds.

Square footage and pricing are subject to change after templating.

Full payment is required to place the order.

Characteristics of Quartz

Quartz is a man-made surface manufactured from natural quartz, resins, and pigments.

Variations in tonality and pattern are normal between pieces and from lot to lot.

Thickness is nominal and may vary ±3mm.

Seams are required and may vary ±2mm. No seam is invisible.

Quartz Care and Maintenance

  • Do not place hot trays, pans, or pots directly on quartz. If it is too hot for your hand, it is too hot for the surface. Always use trivets.
  • Always use a cutting board; do not cut directly on quartz surfaces.
  • Wipe spills promptly. Avoid prolonged contact with concentrated liquids, chemicals, or dyes, which may stain or discolor.
  • The customer is solely responsible for ongoing care and maintenance of quartz surfaces.

Stainless Steel Sink Care Policy

Essential Care Tips

  • Wipe the sink dry after each use to prevent water spots.
  • Use a protective bottom grid to minimize scratches.
  • Clean regularly with a non-abrasive cleanser or a baking soda paste.
  • For hard water stains, use vinegar. Buff the sink with club soda or olive oil for extra shine.
  • Always clean with a soft cloth or non-abrasive scouring pad, rubbing in the direction of the steel grain.

What to Avoid

  • Do not use cleaners containing chlorides (e.g., chlorine, ammonia, bleach) — these damage the sink's protective layer.
  • Do not use steel wool, abrasive sponges, or metal brushes — they can scratch the surface and cause rust.
  • Avoid leaving rubber mats, wet sponges, rags, or cast iron cookware in the sink for extended periods — these can trap moisture and cause stains or discoloration.
  • Do not use oven cleaners, harsh chemicals, or other corrosive products. If these come into contact with the sink, rinse thoroughly and dry immediately.

Rust Removal

For minor rust or stains, use a mild cleaner such as Bar Keepers Friend with a soft cloth, rubbing along the direction of the grain. Rinse thoroughly and dry immediately to prevent water spots or residue buildup.
